Writing Good Prompts (Context + Task + Constraints)

Example:
"I'm writing a proposal for a DoD contract (context). Write the opening paragraph of the technical approach (task). Keep it under 200 words and mention our cleared facility (constraints)."
ContextWhat Claude needs to know about your situation
TaskWhat you want done (be specific!)
ConstraintsBoundaries - length, format, what to include/avoid

The Expert Frame - Tell Claude What Role to Play

"You are an experienced proposal reviewer..."Gets critical feedback
"You are a federal contracting expert..."Gets compliance focus
"You are a marketing strategist..."Gets promotional angle

The role you assign changes the type of response you get!

When Things Go Wrong

Claude confusedStart a fresh session
Same error repeatingTry a different approach
20+ back-and-forthsStart over with fresh context
Output too genericAdd more specific details

Two-Session Debugging

When stuck in Cowork:

  1. Open Claude.ai in browser (second session)
  2. Paste your conversation
  3. Ask: "Why isn't this working?"

Fresh Claude sees problems the stuck Claude can't!

The Iteration Loop (Make Claude Improve Its Work)

1.Get initial output
2."Review this against [criteria]. What's weak?"
3."Fix those weaknesses."
4."Is this good enough, or what else needs work?"

Don't accept the first draft - have Claude critique and improve it!

Common Mistakes to Avoid

MistakeInstead, Do This
Treating Claude like GoogleGive it specific material to work with
Expecting memory between sessionsRe-provide context each time
Giant prompts with everythingBuild up through conversation
Saying "make this better"Be specific: "Add more examples" or "Shorten to 100 words"

⚠️ Cowork Safety

Only grant access to work foldersNot your whole computer
Don't give access to sensitive filesPasswords, credentials, personal docs
Monitor what Cowork is doingStop if it accesses unexpected things

You're responsible for Cowork's actions. Review before it publishes or modifies data.
